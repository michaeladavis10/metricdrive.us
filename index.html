Is Your Business Sellable? An Education.

You're curious about selling, maybe brokers have started reaching out. The first step is to understand what buyers actually value.

The Buyer's Perspective

Buyers aren't buying your past success; they are buying your future profits and taking on all the risk.

Here is what they look for (and what they penalize for):

✔ Owner Dependency: If the business only runs because you are there, its value is minimal. Can you take a 4-week vacation?
✔ Systems & SOPs: Are processes documented? Or is everything in your head? Buyers pay a premium for systems that are proven to work.
✔ Clean Financials (Buyer's View): A transition isn't apples-to-apples. I'll show you how a buyer really models your financials, including their debt and risk. This gets you to a real "market value," not just a "paper value."
✔ SBA Financeability: Are your books clean enough to get an SBA loan? If not, your buyer pool is tiny, and your price will reflect that.
✔ Risk Abatement: Do you have long-term contracts? Is 80% of your business from one client? High customer concentration is a massive risk flag that lowers value.
✔ Defensibility: Is the business recession-proof? AI-proof? What are the barriers to entry that stop a new competitor?

The Seller's Perspective

You need to define what you want from an exit:

✔ Your Legacy: Do you care about your employees and customers? Do you want your name to live on, or just take the cash?
✔ Post-Sale Life: Do you want to walk away clean? Consult? Stay on as a license-holder (RMO - Responsible Managing Officer)? We can structure a deal for any of these.
✔ Financial Outcomes: We'll model your scenarios. What does a "liquidation" or "asset-only" sale look like versus a "best-case" strategic sale? Understanding this informs your negotiation.
✔ Tax & Deal Structures: A cash-only sale has massive tax implications. We can explore creative structures (seller notes, upside equity) that can put more in your pocket.
✔ Risk & Reward: Are you willing to bet on the future upside? Your risk appetite determines the deal structure.

Do You Need a Perfect Score?

No. If your business had all of this, you'd already have big offers on the table. Most businesses don't. Some of these things, like customer concentration, can't be changed without fundamentally changing your business.

The goal isn't to be perfect; the goal is to understand what type of buyer is best suited for your business. From there, we can dig into the trade-offs you're willing to make to maximize your exit.
