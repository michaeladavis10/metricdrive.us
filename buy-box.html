Acquisition Criteria & Buyer Profile

For brokers and sellers: criteria, thesis, and contact.

Michael Davis

MetricDrive

mdavis@metricdrive.us

916-581-0056

I'm acquiring an accounting firm and building into it the technical infrastructure layer most CPA firms can see the value in but don't have the skill to build -- giving clients something their accountant has never been able to offer them.

Thesis

Buy a stable compliance practice, then connect operational and marketing data to the books. Target clients are operating companies with systems that should tie to the GL, or high-net-worth households that need one picture across entities and activities. Most of that work still lives in spreadsheets; the goal is to replace ad hoc reconciliation with repeatable pipelines.

  • ▸ For operating companies: wire job costing, field/service platforms, inventory, and commission or lead-cost logic into the general ledger so margin is visible in the books, not only in side files.
  • ▸ For individuals and families with several streams (rentals, pass-throughs, solopreneur income, multiple accounts): consolidate reporting so cash flow and tax posture are clear in one place, not a separate folder per activity.

What changes for the client: numbers they can use on a normal reporting cadence, not only a PDF package weeks after month-end.

II. Industry Targets

Primary: accounting, bookkeeping, and tax firms with substantive small-business and/or high-net-worth work (multi-entity complexity, operational systems to tie out), not firms built mainly on volume-only individual prep. Secondary: data-intensive services if the fit is obvious; specialty trades only in the narrow case in that card.

Primary

Accounting, Bookkeeping & Tax

• Key Qualifiers & Deal Terms

A compliance-focused bookkeeping and accounting firm with $300K-$500K+ SDE, a staff that owns client relationships directly, and a client base that includes businesses or individuals whose financial complexity has outgrown what a standard CPA practice can handle. The tell: the seller has been asked for reporting, dashboards, or system integrations they couldn't provide. That gap is where I operate. Sacramento area preferred; fully virtual considered.

  • ▸ General manager or strong second-in-command in place.

IV. Buyer Profile

Operator & scale-up experience

Ran one franchise unit. Fifteen years on data infrastructure at Snowflake, LinkedIn, and TripAdvisor (acquisitions/IPOs). Consulting for PE firms on HVAC acquisitions: built reporting from dispatch, ticket, and revenue data so buyers could verify revenue and size the deal.

Education & licenses

  • ▸ M.S., Applied Mathematics & Statistics
  • ▸ Corporate Finance Institute (CFI), Financial Modeling & Valuation Analyst (FMVA). Full FMVA track: financial modeling in Excel (three-statement models, scenarios), valuation (DCF, comparable company analysis), and accounting and financial statement analysis from a finance perspective.
  • ▸ CTEC Registered Tax Preparer
  • ▸ Additional certifications in AI and supply chain optimization

Current role

I run DataBuddy (data consulting). Habitat electrical crew volunteer.

Buyer type

Self-funded principal. I do real diligence before I wire money. Post-close I am in the business with the team on operations and growth, not a strip-and-flip.

What I Bring That Others Don't

Most accounting firm buyers are CPAs absorbing a book into their existing practice, or PE groups running an efficiency play. I'm neither. I spent 12 years in data engineering at companies like Snowflake, LinkedIn, and TripAdvisor before becoming an operator. I understand financial compliance and I can build the technical infrastructure that sits above it -- automated reporting, system integrations, consolidated views across entities. For clients who have been asking their CPA for something and been told it's not possible, I'm the answer to that question. That's not a feature I'm adding. It's the reason I'm acquiring.

V. Deal Structure

Post-Close Commitment

The staff stays. The brand stays. The compliance work continues exactly as clients expect it. What changes is that the firm gains a technical capability it didn't have before -- one that becomes available to clients who want it, on their timeline, without disrupting anything that's already working. I'm not here to strip this down or scale it offshore. I'm here to build on what the seller created.

Deal Mechanics

Seller financing preferred, with structure tied to client retention through transition. Open to SBA 7(a) where deal size warrants it. Earnout or rollover equity welcomed where the seller wants ongoing participation. Target LOI by June 2026, close by Q3 2026. Pre-qualified and ready to move.

License Requirements

Licensing Status

I do not hold a CSLB (contractor) or CPA license.

Contractors

Same narrow case as above: I am not CSLB-licensed. Seller stays on the license until the board allows a handoff; note, earnout, or rollover to match.

Financial services

CTEC registered. RIAs and CPAs on call where a license is required. I own the business; licensed people deliver regulated work.
