Articles

Online guides and articles to help you navigate the exit process.

When to Use SDE vs EBITDA: A Guide for Business Owners

DealCoach

A comprehensive guide explaining the difference between SDE and EBITDA, when to use each metric, and how valuation multiples typically range from 2-5x depending on business structure. Includes breakdowns by industry and buyer type (individual buyers, strategic buyers, financial buyers) to help you understand how your business will be valued.

The 4 Types of Buyers of Businesses

Morgan & Westfield

A comprehensive guide to understanding individual buyers, financial buyers, strategic buyers, and industry buyers. Learn how to identify which type is most likely to purchase your business and how to market accordingly.

View Article

Exiting Your Business

BizBuySell

A great, free online guide from one of the largest marketplaces. It covers the basics of valuation, finding a broker, due diligence, and closing the deal.

SBA Loan Requirements for Business Acquisitions

SBA.gov

Understanding SBA financing is critical since most small business acquisitions rely on SBA loans. This guide covers eligibility, requirements, and how to structure a deal that's financeable.

Due Diligence Checklist for Business Buyers

A practical guide to the due diligence process from a buyer's perspective. Understanding what buyers look for helps you prepare your business for sale and anticipate their concerns.

The Art of the Exit: Preparing Your Business for Sale

Key strategies for preparing your business for sale, including financial preparation, operational improvements, and timing considerations. Learn what to do 2-3 years before you want to exit.

Books

Essential reading for understanding business value and the exit process.

Note: There are no affiliate links or kickbacks on any of these recommendations. These are simply resources I've found valuable.

The E-Myth Revisited

Michael E. Gerber

The single best book on the difference between "working *in* your business and working *on* your business." This is the foundation for building real, sellable value.

View on Amazon

Built to Sell

John Warrillow

A parable that walks you through the process of turning a service-based business (which is dependent on you) into a productized, scalable asset that someone will want to buy.

View on Amazon

Simple Numbers, Straight Talk, Big Profits

Greg Crabtree

You can't sell what you can't measure. This book is a no-nonsense guide to understanding your financials from a *cash-flow* perspective, which is exactly how buyers will look at you.

View on Amazon

HBR Guide to Buying a Small Business

Richard Ruback & Royce Yudkoff

The single best way to learn how to sell your business is to learn how smart people *buy* them. This is the textbook for "Search Fund" buyers and will show you exactly what they look for.

View on Amazon

Buy Then Build

Walker Deibel

Another "think like a buyer" book. This one is geared toward individual entrepreneurs and operators. It shows how they analyze deals, structure financing, and mitigate risk.

View on Amazon

How to Buy a Good Business at a Great Price

Richard Parker

A comprehensive guide offering step-by-step instructions for purchasing a business, covering everything from valuation to negotiation. Essential reading for understanding the buyer's perspective.

Visit Website

Traction: Get a Grip on Your Business

Gino Wickman

The foundational book of the Entrepreneurial Operating System (EOS). Learn how to build a self-managing, scalable business through six key components. Essential for creating a sellable business.

View on Amazon
